Critical Thinking

The mental process of actively and skillfully conceptualizing, applying, analyzing, synthesizing, and evaluating information to reach an answer or conclusion. Strategy: A carefully devised plan of action to achieve a goal, or the art of developing or carrying out such a plan. Logic: The science dealing with the principles of valid reasoning and argument.

Economics & Finance

The social science that deals with the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services and with the theory and management of economies or economic systems. Finance: The business or art of managing the monetary resources of an organization, country, or person. Ecosystems: A localized group of interdependent organisms together with the environment that they inhabit and depend on.

The ability to guide, direct, or influence people. Management: The act, manner, or practice of managing; handling, supervision, or control.

The process of making choices or reaching conclusions. Consequences: Understanding how something logically or naturally follows from an action or condition.

Mathematics & Science

The study of the relationships among numbers, shapes, and quantities. It uses signs, symbols, and proofs and includes arithmetic, algebra, calculus, geometry, and trigonometry. Science: A systematically organized body of knowledge about a particular subject.

Geography & Environment

The study of all the physical features of the Earth's surface, including its climate and the distribution of plant, animal, and human life. Environment: The air, water, minerals, organisms, and all other external factors surrounding and affecting a given organism at any time.

The past events of a period in time or in the life or development of a people, an institution, or a place. Diversity: Differing one from another. Made up of distinct characteristics, qualities, or elements. Culture: The beliefs, customs, practices, and social behavior of a particular nation or people.

Communication & Collaboration

The imparting or interchange of thoughts, opinions, or information by speech, writing, or signs. Collaboration: The act of working together with one or more people in order to achieve something.

System Thinking

Systems thinking is a framework that is based on the belief that the component parts of a system can best be understood in the context of relationships with each other and with other systems, rather than in isolation. Semiotics: The study of signs and symbols as elements of communicative behavior; the analysis of systems of communication, as language, gestures, or clothing.
